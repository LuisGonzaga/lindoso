Lindoso Castle

The Border Watchtower

Lindoso Castle is a very important miltary Portuguese monument, by its strategic location (looking over Rio Lima's flow that is also the Portuguese border with Spain, and enabling a travellers inner line between the mountains of Peneda and Gerês), but also because of technique and style introduced with its construction and influence in the Medieval Portuguese Military Architecture.

There is some controversy about the name Lindoso. Some experts claim it comes from the Latin word "Limitosum" (limiter/frontier), but the toponym doesn't exist in the 1220 General Inquests. This was an administrative procedure used by the monarchy that determined the possession of lands that could be owned by the Crown, nobles or the Church.

But there is also a legend that king D.Diniz, once travelling through the north region of the country, thought that Lindoso would be an excellent place to defend the Portuguese frontier that suffered from burglars from the other side of the river. When the work was finished the king was very impressed with such beauty, and he claimed in a deep emotion " so joyful and absolute I find you, that immediately Lindoso I called you". Beautiful is equal to Lindo, Lindo+so is something like beauty in movement).

1662

Following the Restauration wars, Lindoso's castle is taken by Spanish troops under the command of general Baltazar Pantoja. Dates from this period the building of bullwark fences, designed by the Military Engineer D. Gasparo Squarciáfico, Marquis of Buscayolo.

1664

Portuguese troops reconquer Lindoso's Castle, under the orders of Capitan Carlos Malheiro Pereira being at the time the Governour of Minho D. Francisco de Sousa, Count of Prado.

19

1856

On the day of 18 of August the "Comissão Mista de Demarcação de Limites entre Portugal e Espanha" (mixed comission to delimit the borders between Pprtugal and Spain) picks for the first time the question of drawing the border in Lindoso, a controversial process because of the Spanish pretentions regarding Monte da Madalena.

1895

The last military garrison abandons the castle, deactivating Lindoso's fortress.

20

1932

It is granted to the Castle the status of Portuguese National Monument.
